Company Overview

What OnPoint Does
OnPoint Industrial Services is the safety and logistics backbone for large industrial plants — oil refineries, petrochemical facilities, and similar sites across all 50 states. They specialize in turnarounds (massive, scheduled plant shutdowns), providing safety oversight, confined-space monitoring technology, materials management, and workforce coordination. Revenue comes from service contracts, technology licensing (proprietary WatchPoint and TrackPoint systems), and managed logistics fees. Founded in 2005, grown from $15M to $225M under PE ownership. 95% labor-based business; staffing and workforce allocation are the operational core.

Golden Record — Discussion Guide
Timothy's three questions, fully answered

Timothy explicitly asked about three things regarding the Golden Record. Here is a prepared answer for each:

Question 1: "How do we clean that up in the historical data?"

Prepared Answer
"MDM uses probabilistic and deterministic matching to compare records across every source system you connect to it. For ExxonMobil Beaumont specifically, it would find all 7 variants, match them based on configurable rules (industry, location, relationship data, etc.), and merge them into one Golden Record. The historical data in Salesforce, Sage Intacct, Bullhorn — it all gets updated to point to the same master. You're not deleting history; you're connecting it."

Question 2: "How do we set the new 'standard'?"

Prepared Answer
"You configure survivorship rules — the business logic that decides which value wins when two records conflict. For customer name, you might say 'prefer the Salesforce value' or 'prefer the most recently updated' or 'prefer the value that matches the ZoomInfo enrichment you already have.' You own the rules. MDM enforces them automatically, every time a new record is created or updated."

Question 3: "How do we ensure it is managed to that standard from quote (SF) to cash (Workday) going forward?"

Prepared Answer
"Once the Golden Record exists, MDM publishes a master record ID. That ID is the shared key between Salesforce and Workday. A quote in SF references ID 00041 — ExxonMobil Beaumont. When that job moves to Workday, it carries ID 00041. When actuals are posted in Workday, they're tagged to ID 00041 and can flow back to Salesforce automatically. Any new record that tries to enter either system gets checked against the Golden Record before it's created — the guardrails Timothy described are built into the transaction."

Handling Ben (IT Leadership)
He is there to protect Workday decisions already made. Don't threaten them.
⚠️ Critical Guidance — Read Before the Meeting
Timothy explicitly said Ben is there "to provide us a reality check from the current state of our Workday implementation and the ballast of IT leadership." Ben's job in this room is to pump the brakes on anything that conflicts with IT decisions already made. If you accidentally position MDM as overlapping with, replacing, or complicating Workday, Ben will use it to kill the deal.
  • Never say: "MDM will manage your customer data in Workday"
  • Never say: "MDM replaces what Workday is doing for master data"
  • Never say: "You'll need IT to make changes to Workday for this"
  • Always say: "No source system changes required — Workday stays exactly as-is"
  • Always say: "MDM governs the layer between systems — it's additive to the IT architecture"
  • Always say: "MDM makes the Workday launch better — you're starting Finance on Jan 1 with a clean customer master instead of today's fragmented one"

How to Turn Ben Into an Ally

Strategy
Ben's core concern is likely: "We're already deep in Workday. I don't want another data project complicating our go-live." The pivot is to make MDM look like something that protects the Workday investment rather than threatening it.
  • "Ben, you're going live with Workday Finance on January 1. The question MDM answers is: what master customer data does Workday inherit on that day? Right now the answer is the same fragmented, 7-variant customer master that exists today. MDM ensures Workday inherits one clean, governed record for every customer — from day one. That makes your go-live better, not harder."
  • If he pushes back on timeline: "MDM doesn't require Workday to do anything differently. The integration runs at the data layer — Workday's configuration stays untouched."
  • If he asks about integration: "We have pre-built connectors for Workday. Joe Hartmann would be the right person to go deeper on the technical architecture — that's part of why we'd love to get Joe in the extended session."
Likely Ben Objection
"We've already made our MDM decisions as part of the Workday implementation. We're using Workday's native capabilities."
Response: "Workday has data management capabilities within its modules — that's the right call for operational data within Workday. What Informatica MDM addresses is the cross-system problem: governing the customer identity that needs to be consistent between Salesforce, Workday, Bullhorn, and your legacy systems. Workday can't own the Salesforce records; Salesforce can't own the Workday records. MDM is the neutral master that both reference. They're complementary, not competing."
