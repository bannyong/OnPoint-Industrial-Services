Company Overview
≤ 100 words
What OnPoint Does
OnPoint Industrial Services is the safety and logistics backbone for large industrial plants — oil refineries, petrochemical facilities, and similar sites across all 50 states. They specialize in turnarounds (massive, scheduled plant shutdowns), providing safety oversight, confined space technology, materials management, and workforce coordination. Revenue comes from service contracts, technology licensing (their proprietary WatchPoint and TrackPoint systems), and managed logistics fees. Founded in 2005, grown from $15M to $225M under PE ownership. 95% labor-based business; staffing and workforce allocation are the operational core.

The window to do this right is before Workday Finance lands — not after." Timothy acknowledged on the call that full implementation is a 2027 initiative — but also said "it's possible and wise to start conversations as early as possible."
Likely Objection
"We're cash-constrained right now."
Response: Timothy said verbatim: "We are open to discussing any commercial arrangement that's beneficial." This was said in response to Dylan raising deferred payments. Come prepared with a phased option: start with customer domain only, prove value, expand before exit. Frame it as: the cost of not doing this is a lower exit multiple.
Likely Objection
"We don't really have a duplicate problem — our numbers are accurate."
Response: Agree with them. "You're right — Timothy said as much on our last call, and we believe it." MDM isn't about fixing wrong numbers — it's about making accurate numbers accessible and aggregatable. One job showing up in three places because of naming conventions isn't a data accuracy problem — it's a governance problem MDM is purpose-built to solve.
Likely Objection
"Won't this require big IT involvement we don't have bandwidth for?"
Response: "MDM doesn't require any source system to change. Salesforce, Sage Intacct, Workday, and Bullhorn all stay exactly as they are. Informatica handles the integration layer. Joe's team would be involved in implementation design, but the day-to-day burden stays low — if we do our job well, your reps won't even know Informatica is there."
